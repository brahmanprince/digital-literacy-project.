# Stay Safe Online: A Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students

**Financial & UPI Safety:**
1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money.** A PIN is only required when money is being deducted from your account. If an app prompts for a PIN to "accept" a payment, it is a scam.
2. **Double-check the VPA before paying.** Always verify the receiver's name and Virtual Payment Address (VPA) before transferring funds or scanning QR codes at local campus shops.

**General Account & Device Security:**
3. **Beware of public Wi-Fi.** Avoid logging into sensitive portals (bank accounts, primary emails) on open college or cafe Wi-Fi networks without routing your traffic through a trusted VPN.
4. **Enforce Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).** Enable an authenticator app (like Aegis or Google Authenticator) for your college email, GitHub, and social media accounts. 
5. **Keep your system updated.** Consistently run system updates on your Arch Linux machine and mobile devices to ensure all software vulnerabilities are patched.
6. **Scrutinize urgent university emails.** If you receive an email demanding immediate fee payment or threatening account suspension, check the actual sender address, not just the display name. Verify by logging directly into the official portal.
7. **Reject unsolicited job offers.** Be highly suspicious of WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering easy part-time typing or rating jobs that require you to pay an upfront "registration" or "security" fee. 
8. **Audit your digital footprint.** Keep your personal social media profiles private and avoid sharing excessive details about your daily routine or exact campus location.
 **EMERGENCY REPORTING CHANNELS**
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cybercrime or financial fraud, report it immediately:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Cybercrime Helpline Number:** Dial **1930** (Available 24x7)
